Welcome to the Map of Video Games

Use the search box to locate your favorite games on the map, or zoom in and look around.

This project was heavily inspired "A visual book recommender", made by Nathan Rooy. Please see his website for more details on the process.
I decided to adapt this concept to video games, using the Steam best sellers list to get the necessary reviews and Steams icons for the images.
I only used the reviews of users with more than 5 reviews, and only video games that had at least 100 reviews from these users. I then used Pointwise mutual information to define how similar two games are, but unlike Nathan, I used the PMI-k family with k=2. This is because PMI is biased towards games that share only a small amount of reviews (for example, there is one reviewer who reviewed both and rated them both positively). By squaring the combined probability, this removes some of that bias. Then I defined two games to be attracted when their PMI is more than -10, and repelled otherwise.
Afterwards, I used a Siamese Neural Network to create a 64D map of video games. After using the t-SNE algorithm to create a 2D map. The map results then from removing the overlap of images, and is displayed using Leaflet after creating image tiles for different zoom levels.

I have tried to remove NSFW games using the store tags, though this unfortunately has also removed some other games, like The Witcher 2, which has Mature as its third tag.
